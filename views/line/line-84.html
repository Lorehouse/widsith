Translation


WIth the Moids I was and with the Perse, and with the Myrgings

Named Entities

  • Moide: Malone 183: Moide are the inhabitates of Møn (tgn 7003643, http://vocab.getty.edu/page/tgn/7003574) in the Danish archipelago, and a well known harbor and battle-site. Earlier editors associated the Moide with the Medes but this spelling would be a one-off then, as the Medes are usuall spelled Meðas or Mœðe.
  • Perse: because of the exotic peoples listed in the previous sentence, these often get translated to the Medes and the Persians; however, Malone points out that in this couplet, they are actually paired with Scandinavian and Germanic tribes within the sentence. Because of this, the identity of the Perse is unknown.
